# Wilson's Goals

_Last updated: 2026-03-19_
_Review cadence: Weekly nudge (Sunday evening), contextual nudges when relevant_

## Personal

### 1. Be a better husband and father
- Present, not just physically there
- Small daily signals to Julia (specific texts, taking the kids without being asked, first 5 min home matter)
- Quality time with the toddler — floor bed transition is the current project
- Share the load with the newborn
- **Measure:** Did Julia feel seen today? Did I take initiative or wait to be asked?

### 2. Do new things for entertainment (time-scarce version)
- Movies, restaurants, experiences — but realistic for a family with a 2yo and 2mo
- Prioritize things that work as a couple or solo in short windows
- Build a running list of "things to try" and actually do them
- **Monthly solo date:** 2 hours, just Wilson. Restaurant, jazz bar, museum, movie — anything new. Book a nanny, put it on the calendar. Non-negotiable.
- **Measure:** Did I do something new this week, even small? Did the solo date happen this month?

### 3. Be a better listener
- In conversations with Julia, colleagues, reports — listen to understand, not to respond
- Put the phone down when someone is talking
- Ask follow-up questions instead of jumping to solutions
- **Measure:** Self-awareness check. Did I catch myself interrupting or half-listening?

### 4. Be fit — aesthetically appealing physique
- Current: PPL split, recomp goal
- Fix the program gaps (identified: too much isolation, not enough compounds, sleep training the toddler will help recovery)
- Progressive overload — track lifts, increase weight/reps systematically
- Nutrition matters more than training for aesthetics — are you tracking?
- **Measure:** Physique progress photos monthly, lift numbers trending up

## Professional

### 5. Act more like a leader
- Wilson heads a big team across multiple desks (discretionary macro, quant/ARP, external managers, strategic portfolio)
- Shift from "best individual contributor" to "force multiplier"
- Set direction, remove obstacles, develop people — not just do the work yourself
- **Measure:** Are your direct reports growing? Can the team function when you're not in the room?

### 6. Be more pragmatic in decisions
- Avoid analysis paralysis — good decisions made fast > perfect decisions made late
- Set decision deadlines. If you're still deliberating after X hours, decide and move
- Accept that some decisions will be wrong. Speed of correction > speed of initial decision
- **Measure:** Are decisions getting stuck? Are you the bottleneck?

### 7. Make the team more productive and communicate better
- Clear expectations, fewer meetings, better async communication
- Are people aligned on priorities or working on different things?
- Regular 1x1s that are actually useful (not status updates — those should be written)
- **Measure:** Does the team know what matters this week without you telling them every morning?

## Nudge Schedule
- **Sunday evening:** Weekly reflection — what moved forward, what didn't, what's the focus for next week
- **Contextual:** When conversations naturally connect to a goal, reference it
- **Monthly:** Broader check-in — are these still the right goals? Anything to add/remove?
