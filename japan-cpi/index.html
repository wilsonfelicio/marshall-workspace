Key Takeaways

Headline inflation rebounded to 1.5% in March from a near four-year low of 1.3% in February, driven primarily by higher fuel and transportation costs.


Core CPI accelerated to 1.8% but remains below the BoJ's 2% target, complicating any near-term rate hike narrative.


Underlying inflation is sticky: Core-core at 2.4% shows services and goods ex-food/energy remain elevated, even as headline moderates from 2025's 3%+ levels.


Food remains the hottest category at 4.0% nationally (Feb), though Tokyo's March data shows processed food cooling to 4.9% from 5.5%.


Energy is deflationary: Tokyo energy fell -7.5% YoY in March. Government subsidies continue to suppress utility costs.


Education deflation persists at -5.6%, reflecting free high school tuition policy effects.


Communication costs surging at +6.8%, the fastest-rising category after food — driven by mobile plan repricing.

Sources: Statistics Bureau of Japan, Trading Economics, MacroMicro — Data as of April 23, 2026. March national category breakdown pending (releases Apr 30).
